# Scenario 04 — SKU Performance Analysis (Hands-On)

**Stack:** Excel  •  **Duration:** ~3h 0m  •  **Maps to sub-scenarios:** 5, 6, 7, 8

## Overview

Work with real SKU sales data to classify products into performance tiers, build composite scoring models, and investigate anomalies like high-performing SKUs with low store presence. This hands-on scenario transforms you from a passive analyst into someone who can make data-driven SKU assortment recommendations.

## Pre-requisites

- Completed: SKU Profiling & Performance Analytics masterclass (Position 3)
- Excel: Pareto charts, percentile calculations, conditional formatting

## Learning Objectives

1. Build a Pareto chart and classify 50+ SKUs into Hero, Core, Tail, and Zombie categories.
2. Create a composite scoring model combining three normalised KPIs.
3. Investigate and explain counterintuitive tier assignments (e.g., premium SKU in L2 with low presence).
4. Calculate seasonal indices and propose a de-seasonalisation strategy for the recommendation engine.

## Deliverables (commit into this folder)

- `pareto-classification.xlsx` — Pareto chart + Hero/Core/Tail/Zombie classification.
- `composite-scoring.xlsx` — 3-KPI normalised composite score and L1–L4 tiers.
- `tier-investigation.md` — written analysis of 3+ counterintuitive tier assignments.
- `seasonality-analysis.xlsx` — seasonal indices and de-seasonalisation proposal.

## Submission Checklist

- [ ] Pareto chart shows the 80/20 split clearly
- [ ] Tier distribution roughly matches percentile design (25% per quartile)
- [ ] At least 3 anomalies investigated with business reasoning
